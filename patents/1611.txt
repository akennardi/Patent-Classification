Abstract
An apparatus for storing and transporting fragile items. The material is
pliable foam which is cut and shaped in a variety of ways in order to be
used to hold crockery and other common household or commercial
items.

Description
Background
The invention provides an effective solution for the safe and reliable
transport and storage of fragile commercial and domestic items, through
the use of a reusable, durable and lightweight product.
The invention is intended to provide a superior, economical alternative
to common packing materials such as newspaper to safely and securely
relocate and store fragile items.
The product's cut-outs are configured and shaped so that they can
accommodate a variety of different products, without the need to
require numerous configurations of the product. It is not quite one-size
fits-all, but is intended that the one configuration of product will be
sufficiently versatile to be suitable for many common fragile items.
The product eliminates the need to use bubble wrap, newspaper,
cardboard or other temporary or disposable packing materials.
The specific dimensions are shown below.
Technical field
The invention is intended for commercial and domestic use.
Summary
The invention is devised to be a re-usable, durable, lightweight,
versatile, effective and economical form of transporting and storing
fragile items.

The Foamglove comprises seven pieces of open cell polyurethane foam
which are cut to suit certain types and shapes of common fragile items,
in a variety of shapes (particularly cylindrical, conical and thin slits).
These cut-outs can be of a range of sizes to accommodate different
ranges of items and products. The cut-outs in the foam are of such size
and shape that they can snugly house most typical kitchen items such as
glasses, plates, bowls and similar glasswares and crockery. The design
of the cut-outs also suits many other household or other domestic or
commercial items.
The Foamglove is configured with dimensions designed to fit
comfortably inside packing and storage boxes typically used and sold in
the removals, storage and hire industries (i.e.: 60cm x 36cm x 30cm ).
The units are made from open cell, medium density polyurethane
foam. The reason this material was chosen is because it is light, durable
and reusable. In addition, the material:
     has low thermal conductivity and a flame retardant additive;
     provides no food for pests;
     is resistant to chemicals and solvents;
     is resistant to rot;
     has good compressive strength.
Open cell polyurethane foam lasts for years - a conservative estimate
would be three years.
The configuration, design and dimensions are not available in the
market. Current market solutions include cardboard or paper pieces
which are wedged between items, or bubble wrap which is wrapped
around items.

The Foamglove does not require any manipulation, as it is designed for
products to simply slide into their protective and secure housing.
The material from which it is made is superior to the available market
offerings.
This system is much better for the environment than current alternatives
as it lasts a long time and does not require single use paper, plastic or
tape.
The box is not necessarily included in the patent application, as the
foam units can be installed into receptacles of appropriate sizes to house
the foam units.
Either slits or holes are cut out of the pieces of foam (which are sized to
fit snugly into the carrying receptacle), into which plates, bowls, cups or
other fragile items can be neatly and securely stored.
The slots are designed and sized to fit common kitchen items so that the
same storage slot or hole can accommodate various sizes and shapes of
items of a generally similar type. It provides a more secure and efficient
system than wrapping pieces of crockery in paper or bubble wrap, as
each piece will be protected from breakage by the foam surrounding
it. It is also much quicker and more convenient.
The system has application to storage and transport.
There are five separate and distinctive custom cut pieces of foam, some
of them duplicated to accommodate a dinner set of six.
A characteristic of significant value in this system is that the product is
reusable, and may be used on numerous occasions (for as long as the

foam remains intact, which may be years), as opposed to newspaper or
bubble wrap or other wrapping, which has limited re-use application.
There is entrenched value in the price of the product in its longevity of
use.
 The solution is durable, versatile, economical and lightweight.
Ultimately, this system is likely to have primary application for
companies which provide relocation and storage services, consumers or
businesses who are relocating residences or other premises. The
commercial relocation and storage companies may on-licence their use.

  Claims
  The applicant claims protection for the invention which isdistinguished
  by having a patent in respect of configured pieces of foam which are
  shaped, adapted and arranged to facilitate the storage of certain shapes
  and sizes of items. Current systems inthe market consist of:
-    rolled or folded newspaper or similar
-    bubble wrap
-    cardboard shaped to house items.
  This product uses particular types of foam which provide an advantage
  in malleability, durability and reusability, as well as being lightweight and
  capable of being cut to fit the dimensions of boxes normally used and
  sold by removal companies.
  Essential technical features:
-    open cell polyurethane foam
-    configured with cut-outs, dimensions as shown in the diagrams
-    outside dimensions of the product are 58cm x 36cm x 29cm to fit in
  boxes typically used and sold by participants in the removalist, storage
  and transport industry.
  Specific claims:
-    open cell polyurethane foam unit which is cut to size to fit its
  housing, and have cylindrical-shaped cut-outs to house bottles, glasses,
  bowls and other items which are generally of such nature

-     foam unit configured with cone-shaped cut-outs intended to
   accommodate similar shaped objects or objects of differing sizes so that
   the smaller objects can fit nearer the point of the cone
-     foam unit configured with slits intended to fit flatter fragile items such
   as plates
-     foam unit configured with rectangular cut-outs to accommodate
   bottles, vases and other similar items which will be placed horizontally.
   The specifications are as follows:
        The assembly consists of:
0001.    - A piece of foam with round holes cut out of it to fit
   6 cups/mugs/glasses:
   C6 = 26cm x 36cm x 12cm (+ 2cm base) + 6 x 10cm holes.
   Four of these foam pieces will be provided.
0002.    - A piece of foam with slits cut into it to fit large or dinner plates:
   P6al = 34cm x 21cm x 27cm (+ 2cm base) + 6 x 30cm x 2cm slits
0003.    - A smaller piece of foam with slits cut into it to fit 3 small or side
   plates:
   P3 = 24cm x 12cm x 22cm (+ 7 cm base) + 3 x 20cm x 2cm slits. Two
   of these foam pieces will be provided.
0004.    - A piece of foam with a large, deep well in the centre of it to fit
   bowls:
   B6 = 24cm x 24cm x 27cm (+ 2cm base) + 20cm
   diameter circle. Squares of foam sheet will be provided to place
   between each bowl.

0005.     - A piece of foam with a small, deep well in the centre to fit cutlery
   or miscellaneous items.
   CY  =   10cm x 12cm x 27cm (+ 2cm base) + 6cm x 8cm ellipse
0006.   -  A piece of foam with rectangular holes cut out of it to fit 2 bottles:
    GA   =  36cm x 26cm x 10cm (+ 2cm base) + 2 holes: 32cm x 10cm x
   10cm each.
    Four of these foam pieces will be provided.
0007.   -  A piece of foam with round holes cut out of it to fit 6 bottles:
   GB   =  36cm x 26cm x 27cm (+ 2cm base) + 6 x 10cm holes.
   Two of these foam pieces will be provided.
0008.      Every piece has a foam base.
0009.      Each plate sits in a two centimetre wide slot. There is a one
   centimetre divider between each one to protect them.
0010.      Four foam pieces for the cups form a block measuring 52cm x
   36cm x 28cm and fit neatly into a crate.
0011.       Four foam pieces for the bottles form a block measuring 52cm x
   36cm x 28cm and fit neatly into a crate.
0012.     Five foam pieces for the plates and bowls form another block
   measuring 57cm x 36cm x 29cm and also fit neatly into a crate. The
   plastic crate is already in general use. It is used by some removalists
   and hired out by some moving companies. It has a two part hinged,
   interlocking lid and measures 60cm x 36cm x 30cm. It was chosen for
   its durability and reusability.
